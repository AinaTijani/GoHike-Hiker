Popular Hiking Locations in Malaysia

  • Bukit Tabur

    The Dragon’s Backbone (world’s largest quartz ridge), Klang Gates Dam, Melawati Hills

  • Gunung Nuang

    Selangor’s tallest peak (over 1,439m), river crossing, boulder climbing, famous tree

  • Cameron Highlands

    14 different trails, Tea plantations, Mossy Forest, Robinson Waterfall, Mount Brinchang

  • Teluk Bahang

    Forest reserve, Monkey Beach, Kerachut Beach, Muka Head Lighthouse

  • Gunung Ledang

    Legendary mountain, Batu Hampar, river crossing


Hiking Season

Summer Hiking Quick Tips

This summer heat can be quite oppressive, but you shouldn’t let it keep you from getting outside and hiking! Hot temperatures can be a danger, but only if you are unprepared. Keep hydrate, wear light, loose, sweat-wicking clothes, and avoid cotton. Wear a hat & sunscreen, Start your hike early and plan your hike for shade and water.

Winter Hiking Quick Tips

Everything takes longer than you'd expect, so don't try to bite off too many miles. Always check the weather forecast & trail conditions and plan accordingly (which might mean canceling). Invest in quality winter clothing and learn how to properly layer. Stay hydrated & snack frequently

Greenery Hiking Quick Tips

Hiking is a fun, relaxing way to experience the great outdoors with friends and family. When you go on a hike, you get to explore places in nature that the roads cannot reach. Leave your worries about time, work and other stressors far behind and reawaken your sense of wonder. There is nothing like reconnecting with friends or loved ones on a journey through nature.
